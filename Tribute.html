Evangelist Billy Graham

The man sent by God to bring many to Christ!

Evangelist Billy Graham was a great man of God who brought a massive revival to the world with his powerful and heartfelt preaching. He witnessed to the World about Christ for about 7 decades(70 years)

He indeed lived a life worthy of note, and is an inspiration and role model to us all.His impact for the kingdom of God, places him in the league of the great Apostles of the early church.

Life and times of Evangelist Billy Graham:

Early life:
  • November 7 1918- Born in Charlotte, North Carolina.
  • 1934- Aged 16, He attended a series of revival meetings in Charlotte by Evangelist Mordecai Fowler Ham.
  • 1936-Graduated from Sharon High School, and enrolled at Bob Jones college, Cleveland, but left after a few months.
  • 1937- Attended Florida Bible School, and graduated with a Bachelor's degrees in 1940.
  • 1938- Was baptized by church's pastor Rev. Cecil Underwood.Later he was ordained by Rev. Underwood in the St. John's River Association.
  • 1940- Enrolled in the Wheaton college, Illinois, and graduated with a Bachelor's degree in anthropology in 1943.
Career:
  • 1943- Served briefly as a pastor of the First Baptist Church in Western Springs, Illinois, till 1944.
  • 1948- Served as President of Northwestern Bible college, Minneapolis, Minnesota, till 1952.
  • 1949- Invited by the ‘Christ for Greater Los Angeles’ group to preach at their L.A. revival. It was extended for an additional five weeks and with heavy media coverage, he became a national figure.
  • After World War II, He preached against the influence of communism which was threatening America's culture. Thus the people turned to spirituality for comfort and he became their leader and a ray of hope.
  • 1950- Founded the 'Billy Graham Evangelistic Association' ministry along with his colleagues, thus expanding his reach.
  • Contributed immensely to human rights movements, and became one of the closest friend's of Rev. Martin Luther King Jr.
  • Held integrated crusades in Birmingham, Alabama, on Easter 1964 in the aftermath of the Sixteenth Street Baptist Church bombing.
  • Held series of meetings in college campuses; Uni. of Minnesota(1950-1951), Uni. of Yale (1957), Uni. of North Carolina (1982), amongst others.
  • 1984- Led a series of meetings in the United Kingdom, called "Mission England".
  • 1991- Held his largest event in North America on the Great Lawn of New York's Central Park.
Achievements and Awarded honors:
  • 1965- Honored with the American Academy of Achievement's Golden Plate Award and Horatio Alger Award.
  • 1967- Became the first Protestant to receive an honorary degree from Belmont Abbey College, a Roman Catholic school.
  • 1971- Awarded the International Brotherhood Award from the National Conference of Christians and Jews.
  • 1977- Received the American Jewish Committee's First National Inter-religious Award and Southern Baptist Radio and Television Commission's Distinguished Communications Medal.
  • 1983- Rewarded with Presidential Medal of Freedom, the Nation's highest civilian award.
  • 1989- Received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
  • 2000- Received the Ronald Reagan Presidential Foundation Freedom Award, for monumental and lasting contributions to the cause of freedom in 2000.
  • 2001- Awarded the Honorary Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire (KBE) for his international contribution to civic and religious life for over 60 years.
